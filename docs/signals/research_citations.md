# Research Citations & Validation Sources

## Foundational Financial NLP Research

### Sentiment & Tone Analysis

**Loughran, T., & McDonald, B. (2011)**
*When Is a Liability Not a Liability? Textual Analysis, Dictionaries, and 10-Ks*
Journal of Finance, 66(1), 35-65.
- **Key Finding**: Standard sentiment dictionaries fail in financial context
- **Contribution**: Created domain-specific positive/negative word lists
- **Application**: L002, L038 (negativity scoring)

**Loughran, T., & McDonald, B. (2016)**
*Textual Analysis in Accounting and Finance: A Survey*
Journal of Accounting Research, 54(4), 1187-1230.
- **Key Finding**: Comprehensive review of text-based financial analysis
- **Contribution**: Established best practices for financial text analysis
- **Application**: Overall methodology validation

**Tetlock, P. C. (2007)**
*Giving Content to Investor Sentiment: The Role of Media in the Stock Market*
Journal of Finance, 62(3), 1139-1168.
- **Key Finding**: Media pessimism predicts downward market pressure
- **Contribution**: Validated sentiment-return relationships
- **Application**: L001, L003 (sentiment-performance links)

**Huang, X., Teoh, S. H., & Zhang, Y. (2014)**
*Tone Management*
The Accounting Review, 89(3), 1083-1113.
- **Key Finding**: Managers strategically use positive language around negative earnings
- **Contribution**: Documented sentiment manipulation patterns
- **Application**: L003 (management optimism bias)

### Readability & Complexity

**Li, F. (2008)**
*Annual Report Readability, Current Earnings, and Earnings Persistence*
Journal of Accounting and Economics, 45(2-3), 221-247.
- **Key Finding**: Firms with lower earnings have less readable filings
- **Contribution**: Established readability as earnings quality proxy
- **Application**: L013, L014, S001, S010 (complexity metrics)

**Lehavy, R., Li, F., & Merkley, K. (2011)**
*The Effect of Annual Report Readability on Analyst Following and the Properties of Their Earnings Forecasts*
The Accounting Review, 86(3), 1087-1115.
- **Key Finding**: Complex filings lead to less accurate analyst forecasts
- **Contribution**: Linked complexity to information asymmetry
- **Application**: L014, L018 (sentence/syntactic complexity)

**Bloomfield, R. (2008)**
*Discussion of "Annual Report Readability, Current Earnings, and Earnings Persistence"*
Journal of Accounting and Economics, 45(2-3), 248-252.
- **Key Finding**: Managers may intentionally obfuscate bad news
- **Contribution**: Theoretical framework for strategic complexity
- **Application**: L016, L031 (jargon, readability drops)

**Bushee, B. J., Gow, I. D., & Taylor, D. J. (2018)**
*Linguistic Complexity in Firm Disclosures: Obfuscation or Information?*
Journal of Accounting Research, 56(1), 85-121.
- **Key Finding**: Some complexity reflects genuine business complexity
- **Contribution**: Distinguished informative vs. obfuscating complexity
- **Application**: L019 (lexical diversity interpretation)
